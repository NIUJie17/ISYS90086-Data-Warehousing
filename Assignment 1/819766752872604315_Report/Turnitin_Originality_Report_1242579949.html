161. Executive Summary The Overhill winery is a medium-sized boutique winery that sells 3 types (7 brands) of wine. In order to achieve future sales growth, 18Sane Yardman, the managing director of the Overhill winery, believes that the winery needs better management and 28a better understanding of sales trends. The data warehouse can provide high-quality and consistent data from various data sources. Managers can quickly access many data sources and can access 23a large amount of historical data in the data warehouse. This can help managers no longer need 19to rely on limited Data and their intuition to make business decisions. This report focuses on Overhill winery, explaining the 1importance and feasibility of establishing a data warehouse. Based on the winery's original 5the production system and the merchant sales system, a snowflake schema was selected to establish a suitable data warehouse for Overhill winery. This 49report describes the establishment of the Overhill winery data warehouse in five parts. The executive summary is the first part, which briefly introduces key information and suggestions, and lists the report structure. The next part is the relevant content of the data warehouse, which covers the specific concepts 35of the data warehouse and the necessity and benefits of the data warehouse for the enterprise. After that, the 3third part is about the specific design of the Overhill winery data warehouse. This report designed a snowflake schema 67data warehouse for Overhill winery and explained the content and details of related tables 55in the data warehouse. The main content of the fourth part is to show Sane Yardman on 40how to use the data warehouse to solve a series of problems he cares about. The last part is the appendix, which includes the 1data dictionary, SQL statements, and work breakdown. 2. Introduction to data warehousing Definition 32Data warehouse is a subject -oriented, integrated, relatively stable, data collection that reflects historical changes. A data warehouse is not simply a collection of information from different databases. It is a large-scale application system. The data warehouse is based on a large-scale management system, and in this system stores comprehensive data obtained from all the business databases of the enterprise. Through these comprehensive data, users are provided with useful information after processing, and users can analyze it (Golfarelli,2009). The 20data warehouse can be understood in two aspects. The data in the 20data warehouse is integrated into multiple heterogeneous data sources. The integrated data is grouped according to the theme. The data placed in the data warehouse is generally not modified and contains historical data (N. Nataraj,2014). On the other hand, the data warehouse is an analysis-oriented data processing method, which focuses on supporting decision-making rather than dealing with daily business. Necessity With the development of digital technologies and the expansion of demand for data in recent years, it is much more difficult to manage data than before. The main purpose of business database is designed for storing data and basic 9operations. Although it can be used for some business analysis, it requires a lot of readjustment for that. Besides, it still has many 9problems such as complex data structures, data mess, lack of history and slowness for large-scale queries. Gradually, the common business database no longer meets managers’ needs. This is why data warehousing was created. Data warehousing is analysis-oriented and focuses on solving the drawbacks of analysis by business database. For more than storing data and recording history, managers are eager to pick the key information and extract them for decision-making. Those needs for strategic data information motivate the establishment of data warehousing in a great extent (Qin, 2012). Additionally, the granularity of operations is on all integrated data level. It can be accessed easily and provides fast queries for their users. That makes data warehousing more attractive and popular in business analysis. Benefits The major benefit of data warehousing is to help 33managers and executives to make strategic decisions significantly. A data warehousing can provide enhanced business intelligence. They 22will no longer have to make business decisions based on limited data source or their intuition. Data warehousing can be applied in market 22segmentation, inventory management, financial management, sales and other business processes (Donald P, 1999). Another significant 3advantage is that data warehousing can improve the quality and consistency of data. To establish a data warehousing, the data from many other data source systems need to be transferred to a uniform data format. Due to the standardization of data from different departments, data warehousing provides more accurate data and accuracy is the basis of business strategic decisions. 13. Design of the data warehouse In this section, we will introduce and analyze briefly this business case and discuss detailly how we design the data warehouse. Additionally, the fact table 1and dimension tables are introduced separately below. Business case introduction and analysis16Overhill Winery is a medium-sized boutique winery. There are seven types of wines in total. The winery is in charge of 63growing grapes, producing wines and selling them. The wine products 21are sold locally in Melbourne, interstate in Australia and internationally in the UK. The managing director is seeking more further growth based on the grammatic growth in the past three years. Currently, 16there are two separate information systems applied in the business, one is to manage production and the other one is for sales to wine merchants. However, that causes a range of issues about different data formats and database systems. In order to keep a rapid growth, the managing director has to make decisions based on the extracted key information instead of experience and intuition. For this purpose, 47a data warehouse is needed to integrate all different data from two systems and to offer valuable information about customers, sales agents and sales time periods and markets. Overall Design Figure 3.1 design of dimension model As shown in figure 3.1, 23the data warehouse model for Overhill winery is designed as a snowflake schema to help Sane Yardman better understand sales trends and solve the problems that companies are facing. The data warehouse is composed of 7 tables. At the center is 31a fact table called Sale, and there are 5 dimension tables connected to it, which are called market, time, Sales_agent, customer, and product. There is also a dimension table named production connected to 66the product table. The details about each table and the relationship between the tables are described below. Fact table The Sale 3table is the fact table in the snowflake schema, and it is the center table around other dimensions. The Sale table mainly contains sales data of Overhill winery, which is also the business process that Overhill winery focuses on. There are 10 attributes in the Sale table. SalesID 51is the only primary key in the Sale table. A row in the Sale table indicates that a specific customer or agent bought a specific product within a specific time. These products sold to specific customers or agents are sold to a specific market for Overhill winery. This corresponds to 5 foreign keys in the table, namely CustomerRecord, AgentRecord, TimeID, ProductID, and MarketID. The remaining other attributes 54in the fact table are measures. Unit_Sales indicates the sales volume of the product, SalePrice indicates the total cost of the customer in the transaction, Line indicates the order line, and Margin is referring to the profit from this transaction. Dimension tables The Customer dimension 3table is one of the dimensions of Sale fact. The relevant basic information of customers is recorded in this table, such as CustomerID, Firstname, Lastname, and addresses. Key customers can be analyzed from the following aspects. First of all, the proportion of products purchased by men and women should be a direction of analyzing the main customers, so the gender attribute is added. At the same time, the age range of the customer is also a direction that should be paid attention to. We use the DateOfBirth and GroupAge attributes to describe it. The value of Group Age is divided into three intervals 29less than 30 years old, between 30 and 60 years old, and over 60 years old. It is obtained by subtracting the value of the DateOfBirth attribute from the value 19of the time in the Sale fact table. Finally, the area where customers live is also a direction that needs attention, by adding Postcode and Suburb. Because the customer's information may change, 15it is necessary to record both the new data of the customer's change and the historical data of the customer, so add Customer_Recorder 43as the surrogate key to implement, and it is also the primary key of the Customer dimension table. For search convenience, set up CustomerID, GroupAge and Postcode as indexes. The Time dimension table is one of the dimensions of Sale fact. The problem that companies need to solve is 48which time periods are the most profitable. So,1for a specific date, we need to know which day of the week, which month, which quarter, which fiscal quarter, and which year it belongs to. Which week of the year it is, whether it belongs to the first half or the second half. Therefore, a total of 8 attribute implementations have been added, of which TimeID 28is the primary key of The Time dimension table, followed by DayOfWeek, Month, Season, FinQuater, Year, WeekNo, and HalfYear. The 7 attributes correspond to the above description. The Sales_agent dimension table is one of the dimensions of Sale fact. Sales agents are the main sales objects of Overhill winery. One issue that also needs to be analyzed is Who are the key sales agents. So, the basic information of the agents recorded in the table includes AgentID, Agent’Name, and CommissionRate. At the same time, we can analyze the importance of agents from different regions by adding the AgentArea attribute. For the change of Agent, we also need to record new data and historical data, so Sales_agent_record is set as the agent key, and 46it is also the primary key of The Sales_agent dimension table. For quick retrieval, we set AgentID and AgentArea as indexes. The 50Market dimension is a dimension table of the fact table. This table records the MarketID, the MarketName and the MarketRegion. Currently, 36there are three markets, those are Victoria, The rest of Australia and International. As the director requires, this dimension table shows which market each 41unit sales and dollar sales belong to for every month. The market may change in the future, so there should be a surrogate key called Market_sIDto present changes. For retrieving concern, the index is added 38in the MarketID. The Product dimension table is to record all the product relevant information. In this table, the basic information of product is recorded, covering their original id, descriptions, which group they belong to, the cost to produce them, unit price which is also called advised price and the real 56sale price. According to the real sale price and the cost, the margin for each product can be calculated. In order to retrieve, the index is added to the column ProductID. The Production dimension table is to record the information about producing wine process. The ProductionCode is labelled as the primary key in this table. It has two attributes, the Volume is to describe the yearly yield of wines, the YearOfProduction is to record the year of each production. The Production dimension table has a hierarchical relationship with Product dimension table. The quantity of production must be always greater than the quantity of product. So, the ProductionCode in 44this table is a foreign key in Product dimension table. For retrieving purpose, the index in this table is added in the ProductionCode. 4.
